Consider any prime $p > 2$. Clearly $p$ is odd, and this fact is obvious enough
that we will not dwell on it further. We have $p^2 - 1 = (p-1)(p+1)$.
Since $p$ is odd, both $p - 1$ and $p + 1$ are even consecutive even integers,
so one of them is divisible by $4$. Hence $8$ divides $(p-1)(p+1) = p^2 - 1$.
